Our lab is the Harvard Center for Advanced Imaging. We primarily focus on technique and algorithm development for super-resolution fluorescence microscopy using the single molecule localization approach (SMLM).

A complete list of publications is available at Orcid or Google Scholar.



Multiplane and Spectrally-Resolved Single Molecule Localization Microscopy with Industrial Grade CMOS cameras
H. Babcock
Scientific Reports
(In Press)

This paper tested the use of inexpensive (~$1k) CMOS cameras for SMLM. We demonstrated that they perform almost as well as much more expensive scientific CMOS cameras for 2D single plane imaging. We demonstrated the use of these cameras for bi-plane and quad-plane 3D SMLM imaging. We demonstrated a new way to perform SR-STORM using these cameras. We also introduced open-source software for multi-camera data analysis and microscope control.

Analyzing Single Molecule Localization Microscopy Data Using Cubic Splines
 H. Babcock and X. Zhuang
Scientific Reports
7
(2017)

This paper described modeling the microscope PSF with cubic splines. We demonstrated that cubic splines are an accurate, fast and versatile approach for analyzing SMLM movies.

Fast Compressed Sensing Analysis for Super-Resolution Imaging Using L1-homotopy
 H. Babcock, J. Moffitt, Y. Cao, X. Zhuang
Optics Express
21
28583-28596 (2013)

This paper demonstrated that the L1-homotopy algorithm is significantly (10-100x) faster than the CVX algorithm at processing the images typically encountered in dense 2D SMLM movies. L1-H estimates a super-resolution image from the SMLM movie.

A High-Density 3D Localization Algorithm for Stochastic Optical Reconstruction Microscopy
 H. Babcock, Y. Sigal, X. Zhuang
Optical Nanoscopy
1
6 (2012)

This paper introduced the 3D-DAOSTORM algorithm. This is one of the first algorithms that can process 3D astigmatic SMLM movies containing partially overlapping emitters.

Statistical Deconvolution for Super-resolution Fluorescence Microscopy
 E. Mukamel, H. Babcock, X. Zhuang
Biophysical Journal
102
2391-2400 (2012)

This paper introduced the DeconSTORM algorithm. The algorithm incorporates Poisson-distributed photon detection noise, the sparse spatial distribution of activated fluorophores, and temporal correlations between consecutive movie frames arising from intermittent fluorophore activation. It estimates a 2D super-resolution image from a SMLM movie.
